# Surviving Claims (Zero-Weight / Maximal-Skepticism Mode)

These statements remain after every census total, shipping manifest, plantation ledger, and administrative head-count is set to reliability ≈ 0. They are supported by physical remains, genealogical termination patterns, community projects, and the structural silence of the written record itself.

They do **not** underwrite any cumulative population total, precise annual growth rate, or import volume.

---

1. People of African and mixed descent were present on the territory that became the United States for multiple generations prior to 1865.

2. Physical burial grounds and archaeological sites (African Burial Ground NYC, Catoctin Furnace, Anson Street Charleston, Chesapeake Bay 17th-c. sites, and 10+ additional documented locations) document multi-generational presence of these populations on American soil. The bodies themselves constitute independent physical testimony.

3. Genealogical chains for the large majority of documented Black American (FBA) lineages terminate in U.S. records — church registries, county records, plantation logs, Freedmen’s Bureau, and later community projects (Whitney Plantation database, Monticello Getting Word, Georgetown Memory Project, 10 Million Names).

4. Forced labor regimes, family separation practices, legal non-personhood, and anti-literacy statutes existed and left both documentary and physical traces.

5. Racial classification on U.S. censuses and vital records was enumerator-driven (“lookerism” + community reputation + legal-economic incentives) rather than self-reported for most of the historical period. Categories functioned as administrative tools, not neutral demographic accounting.

6. Comparable Caribbean and Brazilian slave societies showed net natural decrease and required continuous imports to maintain population. The U.S. recorded pattern (if the counts are accepted) is an outlier with no global precedent after legal import bans.

7. Ancient DNA sampling of historical populations remains on the order of 0.000017 % of all humans who ever lived and is statistically insufficient, by itself, to ground population-level origin narratives.

8. The majority of quantifiable demographic records were produced by owners, traders, enumerators, or colonial administrators. Systematic pre-1865 testimony from the enslaved population itself is essentially absent (the ~2,300 WPA Slave Narratives represent ~0.02 % of the formerly enslaved and were collected decades later under Jim Crow conditions).

9. Macro-economic externalities of the racial regime (Citigroup 2020 estimate of $16 T cumulative cost; SF Fed/Daly $51 T lost output; Civil War direct costs) demonstrate that micro-profitability for individual enslavers is compatible with macro-catastrophe for the society that produced the records. Records generated by a macro-irrational system carry no automatic presumption of demographic precision.

10. The construct of a binary “Negro/Black” category on American soil, hardened by one-drop rules and the Virginia Racial Integrity Act (1924), created the administrative conditions under which multi-generational lineages were recorded (and sometimes erased) as a single racial group.

---

**Epistemic status**: These claims are qualitative and structural. They survive the zero-weight mode because they do not depend on accepting any particular numerical total as accurate. They are the floor of what can be said with high confidence once the quantitative reconstructions are set aside.
