# Dunbar (1863) — Continental Slave Census Excerpt

**Source**: Edward E. Dunbar, *History of the Rise and Decline of Commercial Slavery in America* (1863). Library of Congress digitized text.

**Provenance**: Secondary compilation of colonial administrative estimates (Spanish, British, French, U.S.).  
**Enslaved voice?**: NO  
**Reliability note**: Administrative aggregate; zero-weight mode treats the head-counts as undefined. The classification distinction (Negro vs. Indian) survives as a qualitative legal fact.

---

**Key table excerpt (p. 201 / logical p. 35)**:

| Territory            | Negro Slaves | Indian Slaves | Total     |
|----------------------|--------------|---------------|-----------|
| United States        | 697,897      | 0 (absorbed)  | 697,897   |
| Spanish Continental  | 410,000      | 7,000,000     | 7,410,000 |
| British Caribbean    | 505,241      | —             | 505,241   |
| French Caribbean     | 800,000      | —             | 800,000   |

**Quote (p. 220 / logical p. 54)**:  
“One stupendous result of these revolutions was the elevation of over 7,000,000 aborigines from the level of brutes to that of human beings.”

**Surviving qualitative signal**: Spanish America maintained separate legal categories for Indigenous enslaved persons; the U.S. binary system did not.
