# SlaveVoyages Transatlantic & Intra-American Databases

**Source**: https://www.slavevoyages.org/  
Estimates: https://www.slavevoyages.org/assessment/estimates  
Intra-American Database: 37,000+ voyages documented.

**Provenance**: Compiled from European and colonial ship manifests, port records, customs documents, and later scholarly imputation for missing voyages.  
**Enslaved voice?**: N/A — enslaved persons were not authors of the manifests.  
**Reliability note**: Primary quantitative source for arrival estimates (~389,000 documented to mainland North America). Zero-weight mode treats the numerical totals as undefined. The existence of continuous intra-American trade and the geographic pattern of voyages survive as qualitative facts.

---

**Key conventional figures** (if accepted):

- Documented African arrivals to mainland North America: ~388,000–400,000 (legal trade).
- Post-1808 illegal + intra-American estimates: commonly 50,000–100,000+ (high uncertainty).
- Intra-American movements (Caribbean → mainland, internal U.S.): significant but harder to quantify precisely.

**Surviving qualitative signals**:
- The 1619 cohort and many later arrivals passed through Caribbean ports first.
- Continuous forced movement of people of African descent within the Americas is documented.
- The U.S. recorded post-ban population trajectory differs sharply from Caribbean and Brazilian patterns that required ongoing imports.
