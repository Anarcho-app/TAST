# Virginia Company Records — 1619 Arrival

**Source**: Virginia Company of London, Records, Vol. 3, p. 267 (Library of Congress).

**Provenance**: Corporate / colonial administrative.  
**Enslaved voice?**: NO  
**Reliability note**: Administrative designation of origin. Zero-weight mode discards any head-count implication; the geographic note (“from West Indies”) survives as a qualitative fact about the 1619 cohort.

---

**Excerpt**:  
The “20. and odd Negroes” are recorded as having arrived “from the West Indies” (not directly from Africa).

**Surviving qualitative signal**: The first documented cohort was already filtered through Caribbean ports and therefore already potentially admixed; the record uses a geographic, not an ancestral, designation.
