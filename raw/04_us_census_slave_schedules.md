# U.S. Federal Census Slave Schedules (1790–1860)

**Sources**:
- U.S. Census 1790: https://www.census.gov/history/pdf/1790slaves.pdf
- U.S. Census 1860 Slave Schedule: https://www.census.gov/library/publications/1864/dec/1860a.html
- Intermediate decades available via IPUMS / National Archives

**Provenance**: Federal enumerator count (visual assessment + owner reports).  
**Enslaved voice?**: N/A — no enslaved input to the census process.  
**Reliability note**: Core quantitative backbone of conventional and reweighted models. In zero-weight mode these head-counts become undefined. The fact that classification was enumerator-driven (“lookerism”) survives as a qualitative structural fact.

**Surviving qualitative signals**:
- Racial classification on the U.S. census was not self-reported for the enslaved population.
- The binary “Negro / White” system differed from multi-category systems used in Spanish America.
- Continuous documentation of a growing population classified as “slave” / “Negro” exists across seven decades on American soil.
