# Stream 29 — Steckel Anthropometric Signature (discriminating)

**Producer**: Shipper-mediated coastwise manifests (post-1808). Incentive is to prove *domestic* origin — opposite undercount pressure to census/owner records.

**Why it can break the H5 deadlock**  
H5 currently wins because almost nothing is surprising under it. Stream 29 supplies a joint morphological prediction that is surprisable.

## Predicted signatures

| Hypothesis | Prediction under Stream 29 |
|------------|----------------------------|
| **H1** (documented arrivals + exceptional NI under U.S. conditions) | Severe childhood stunting, high infant/child mortality markers, then pronounced adolescent catch-up when children entered field labor and rations improved; early age at first birth; short birth intervals. Joint morphological signature. |
| **H2** (classification / absorption on American soil) | No necessary prediction of this stunting→catch-up profile. Classification practice does not imply the anthropometric trajectory. |
| **H3 / H4** | Partial or regional versions of the H1 signature possible; weaker or geographically restricted. |
| **H5** (residual / mixed / undocumented) | Can accommodate the signature or its absence; therefore *can be surprised* if the signature is strongly present or strongly absent in a way that fits only one mechanism. |

## Encoding
- Current point likelihoods (v5.8): H1=0.70, H2=0.35, H3=0.50, H4=0.55, H5=0.45  
- These are provisional analyst judgments pending tighter linkage to published height-by-age and skeletal series.  
- Selection bias in manifests is acknowledged (healthier sample); it does not erase the directional prediction.
